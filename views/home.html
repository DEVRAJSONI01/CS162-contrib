History

Wheat has been a staple food for over 10,000 years, dating back to the earliest civilizations of Mesopotamia, Egypt, and the Indus Valley. Ancient grains were unrefined, packed with fiber, and full of nutrients, forming the foundation of traditional diets across the world. Whole wheat was used to make flatbreads, porridges, and rustic loaves, providing energy and nourishment.

For centuries, people consumed whole wheat, but as milling techniques advanced, refined flour (maida) emerged. In the 19th century, with the introduction of roller mills, wheat grains were stripped of their bran (fiber) and germ (nutrients), leaving behind only the starchy endosperm—creating the fine, white flour we now know as maida.
